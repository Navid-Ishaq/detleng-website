How We Work

From Raw Data to Analytics-Ready Data

At DeTLeng, we believe business intelligence starts long before a dashboard is built.

Most reporting challenges are not caused by dashboards. They are caused by inconsistent data, disconnected systems, unclear business rules, and unreliable metrics.

Our focus is on building the data foundation that makes reporting trustworthy.

We help organizations transform raw, fragmented, and inconsistent data into clean, structured, analytics-ready datasets that support reporting, business intelligence, automation, and decision-making.

Our Data Engineering Framework

Every project follows a structured methodology designed to improve data quality, reporting reliability, and long-term maintainability.

Phase 1 — Business Understanding

Every engagement begins with understanding the business behind the data.

  • Business objectives
  • Reporting requirements
  • Existing processes
  • Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
  • Data sources
  • Operational challenges
  • Desired outcomes

Objective: Understand what the business needs before designing any technical solution.

Phase 2 — Data Assessment & Profiling

Before transformation begins, we evaluate the available data and identify opportunities for improvement.

  • Source system review
  • Spreadsheet assessment
  • Database evaluation
  • Data profiling
  • Data quality assessment
  • Structure and schema review

Phase 3 — Data Cleaning & Standardization

Raw data is rarely analytics-ready. This phase focuses on improving reliability and consistency.

  • Missing value analysis
  • Duplicate detection
  • Data type validation
  • Naming standardization
  • Business rule validation
  • Data consistency checks
  • Error identification

Phase 4 — ETL & Data Transformation

Once data quality issues are understood, transformation workflows are developed.

  • SQL transformations
  • ETL pipelines
  • ELT workflows
  • BigQuery implementations
  • Data integration processes
  • Data enrichment
  • Feature engineering

Raw operational data is converted into business-ready datasets.

Phase 5 — Data Validation

Before delivery, all outputs are validated to ensure reporting accuracy and confidence in business metrics.

  • Record count verification
  • Metric validation
  • Source-to-target reconciliation
  • Business rule testing
  • Data quality testing
  • KPI verification

Phase 6 — Analytics Dataset Development

After validation, analytics-ready datasets are created.

  • Reporting datasets
  • KPI layers
  • Analytics tables
  • Data marts
  • Business metric definitions
  • Executive reporting structures

Phase 7 — Reporting & Visualization (Optional)

Once the data foundation is complete, reporting tools can consume the prepared datasets.

  • Power BI
  • Looker Studio
  • Excel Reporting
  • Executive Dashboards
  • KPI Monitoring Solutions

Because business logic already exists within the data layer, reporting becomes faster, simpler, and more reliable.
